GOOD EXAMPLE
"Join us for Chanukah celebration! Limited spots available. Register now at yu.edu/events or contact admissions@yu.edu"
Why it works: Clear CTA, urgency ("limited spots"), direct link for visual appeal
Engagement: 245 (likes: 197, comments: 34, shares: 14)
POOR EXAMPLE
"We are pleased to announce that our institution continues to uphold the values of academic excellence and community engagement through various initiatives..."
Why it fails: Too formal, no CTA, vague, passive voice, no clear action
Engagement: 58 (likes: 47, comments: 8, shares: 3)
Best Practices for Direct Messaging
  • Use clear CTAs: "Register now", "Apply today", "Learn more", "Join us"
  • Keep it short: 1-2 sentences max, get to the point quickly
  • Add urgency: "Limited spots", "Deadline approaching", "Last chance"
  • Include links: Make it easy to take action with direct URLs
  • Use emojis strategically: 1-2 relevant emojis for visual appeal
  • Avoid jargon: Use simple, conversational language
  • Expected impact: Increase avg engagement from 85 to 197 (+132%) by adopting direct messaging
